<h1 class="page-title">About</h1>


<p>I&#39;m an Astronomer and Instrument Scientist working for the <a href="http://www.esa.int">European Space Agency</a> in Baltimore, MD, as member of the <a href="http://sci.esa.int/jwst/">James Webb Space Telescope</a> (JWST) mission office. JWST is a major next-generation infrared space telescope to be launched in 2018, built as a collaboration between the US, European and Canadian Space Agencies (NASA/ESA/CSA). I specifically work on JWST&#39;s mid-infrared instrument MIRI, and have been a member of the European MIRI consortium since 2007.</p>




<p>I have a PhD in Physics from <a href="http://www.ucl.ac.uk/star">University College London</a> (2006), and have previously held positions at the <a href="http://www.strw.leidenuniv.nl">University of Leiden</a>, Netherlands (2007-2010), the <a href="http://www.mpia.de">Max Planck Institute for Astronomy</a> in Heidelberg, Germany (2010-2013), and the <a href="https://www2.physics.ox.ac.uk/research/astrophysics">University of Oxford</a>, UK (2013-2016).</p>




<p>Looking for my blog? I retired it in mid-2015. If you&#39;re looking for any particular content, <a href="/contact/">email me</a>. A more minimal new blog is <a href="/notes/">here</a>.</p>
